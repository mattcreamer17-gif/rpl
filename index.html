The read

Thirteen years of boutique relationships, at the moment AI is rewriting recruiter economics.

The IT practice is active. Data engineer, senior vulnerability analyst, gaming systems application owner, all in the Miami / Fort Lauderdale market in the last rolling quarter. That is a deliberate build, and it is the part of the book where AI-assisted sourcing already moves faster than a bilingual boutique can run on phone calls and referrals alone.

The nearshore platforms (Near, LatamCent, Freelance Latin America) are pitching thirty to seventy percent cost savings on LatAm placements versus Miami-based hires. The defense is the quality of match and the senior-candidate judgment. Both of those are human layers. Protecting them means documenting them, which is an orchestration problem before it is a tooling problem.

The frame we bring

60 / 30 / 10. Which layer does the work belong on?

We call it the 60/30/10 frame. Applied to a staffing firm, it looks like this. The ten percent at the end is what RPL actually sells, and it is also what a generic AI tool will quietly erode if you let it touch the wrong layer.

60% Traditional code and database

ATS hygiene. Compliance documentation. Scheduling. Basic matching on hard requirements. This layer should sit on deterministic systems and never have touched an LLM in the first place.

30% Rule-based logic

Comp banding. Visa status. Clearance checks. Geography and tax-nexus rules. A rule engine handles this faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees than any language model.

10% Genuine judgment

Calibrating a client's unstated preferences. Reading a candidate's culture fit. Protecting the relationship when a deal goes sideways. This is the part RPL gets paid for, and the part to defend.

Where we think the hours go

The bet is intake, not sourcing.

  1. 01

    Recruiter admin load.

    Industry baseline puts seventy percent of recruiter hours on admin. For a bilingual boutique running dual-language comms across US clients and LatAm candidates, the load is heavier than average. A scoped orchestration layer that drafts candidate summaries, routes intake calls, and auto-populates the ATS gives each recruiter back eight to twelve hours a week without touching the judgment layer.

  2. 02

    Req intake latency.

    Three to seven business days between the client handoff call and the first candidate going out. Most of that is context capture and calibration, not sourcing. An ICM-style intake document that the recruiter fills during the first client call compresses that to one or two days.

  3. 03

    Margin defense against nearshore platforms.

    The LatAm network is now table stakes. The moat is quality of match and senior-candidate judgment. Proving that moat to a skeptical buyer means documenting and measuring it, which is a design problem that happens to look like an engineering problem.

The paper behind the playbook

Interpretable Context Methodology.

Published to ACM TiiS. It formalizes how to organize a firm's operating context so that agents, and humans, work from the same documented reality. For a staffing firm, that context is client history, candidate notes, req patterns, and the tacit knowledge that normally lives inside one recruiter's head.
